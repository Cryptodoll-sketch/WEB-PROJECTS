Book Review

Firestorm is another thrilling installment in Iris Johansen's series featuring forensic sculptor Eve Duncan. This novel takes readers on aheart-pounding journey through danger,mystery, and emotional turmoil.

When a forest fire rages through the California hills, a team of firefighters discovers a body that doesn't belong. Eve Duncan is called in to reconstruct the face of the victim, but what she uncovers is far more sinnister than anyone anticipated.

"Johansen masterfully builds tension page by page, creating a firestorm of suspense that will leave readers breathless until the end."

The narrative weaves together multiple storylines, with Eve's personsal journey intersecting with a dangerous conspiracy that threatens everything she holds dear. As always, Johansen's characters are richly drawn and compelling, with Eve Duncan standing out as a resilient and complex protagonist.

What I Loved

Pacing

The story moves at breakneck speed with perfectly timed reveals that keep you turning pages late into the night.

Character Depth

Johansen creates a palpable sense of danger and urgency, with the firestorm seving as both literal and metaphorical background.

Reader Consideration

While Firestorm can be read as a standalone novel, readers familiar with the Eve Duncan series will appreciate the continuing character development and references to previous events.

The book contains some intense scenes involving violence and danger that may be disturbing to sensitive readers. As with many thrillers, suspension of disbelief is occasionally required for some of the more dramatic plot development.
